MetroGnome




Description:

MetroGnome is a Voxel Rhythm game created by a team of three including myself. The player picks their song, maps out the position of beats to follow, and is thrown into a pleasing field of disintegrating Voxel bullets. It won RIT’s Microsoft Imagine Cup Game Jam and is soon to be showcased at GDC. The game looks to push educational aspects with custom beats created by the player and other educational features.



Role:

As the technical artist I worked on everything related to Art Assets including…

  • Fully Rigged and Animated Character
  • Projection Shaders
  • Lighting
  • Environmental Art
  • User Interface
  • Software: Unity Engine | Maya | Illustrator | Photoshop | MagicaVoxel | Visual Studio
  • Language: C#

ColoRun



Description:

ColoRun is an endless color matching runner. I came up with the idea after falling in love with the simplicity of the original Brown Box Pong. I wanted to create something that could replicate this feeling and I could completely call my own.



Highlighted Features

  • Quasi Random Generated Tiled Field:
    Never have to worry about playing the same game twice because you’ll always have to take a different path.
  • Color Impaired Option:
    I wanted to make this accessible for as many individuals as possible.
  • PowerUps:
    There are 3 different distinct powerups for the player to land on

Play Here / Read More

How to Play:

Quickly and precisely swap between four distinct colors (Red = “A”, Blue = “W”, Yellow = “D”, and Green = “F”) to slide between randomly generated tiles (Using Arrow Keys) and away from the ones disappearing underneath you.

  • Software: Unity Engine | Photoshop | Pyxel | Visual Studio
  • Language: C#

Meshy Tanks


Description:

Meshy Tanks was developed from my weekly educational game jam that followed this process…

  • Figure out what I want to learn or lack knowledge in.
  • Start researching using tutorial videos, forums, and knowledgeable colleagues.
  • Plan out how I can create a game from the newly acquired knowledge
  • Develop the game!

The idea was to expand my knowledge on Game Development elements, by doing research and then creating a game. This week's research was on procedurally generated low poly terrain. I started off learning how meshes were created using triangles and vertices. Then from there I created my own randomly Perlin noise generated terrain. From there I used sodme assets from unity and made a multiplayer tank battle game.



Play Here / Read More

How to Play:

Player 1: "WASD" - Movement | "Space" - Shoot
Player 2: Arrow Keys - Movement | "Enter" - Shoot

  • Date: August 2015
  • Software: Unreal Engine 4
  • Category: Games

Way of The Alley


Description:

Way of The Alley is a 2D art project that was created from a Shmup template. The background of the project was to create a minute cutscene and art assets, including sprites and UI components. I took it a bit farther and rearranged the template to make a top down Shmup runner.

You are a young boy who lost his mother to a gang related robbing years before. They also took your only younger brother. Since that day you’ve been training countlessly to get him back from the ruthless gang that run the alley way.
